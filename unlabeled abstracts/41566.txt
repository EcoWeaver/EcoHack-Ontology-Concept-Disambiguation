Mapping of alien species of Asclepias syriaca and Fallopia japonica populations in the agricultural landscape
In recent years, Asclepias syriaca, a invasive neophyte, has spread aggressively in intensively farmed agricultural landscape in the Central Slovakia. In 2011, it had colonized the area of 100 654 m 2 in abandonment vineyards and permanent grasslands in the district of Velky Krtis (South Slovakia). We found the infiltration of these plants into the stands of Triticum aestivum and Helianthus annuus. No management measures were taken.
