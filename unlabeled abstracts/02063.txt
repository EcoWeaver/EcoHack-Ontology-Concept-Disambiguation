The Invasive Shrub Piper aduncum and Rural Livelihoods in the Finschhafen Area of Papua New Guinea
Bioinvasion is recognized as an important component of the global loss of species and habitats. Some have argued that bioinvasion is a greater threat than the greenhouse effect, industrial pollution or ozone depletion. An understanding of the causes and spread of a bioinvasive species is needed to assess its economic and ecological impacts.
