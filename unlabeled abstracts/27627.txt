Invasive plant management must be driven by a holistic understanding of invader impacts
Understanding impacts of invasive plants is necessary to achieve conservation goals. In this issue of Applied Vegetation Science, Kelemen et al. identify that a widespread invader reduces native abundance, but not richness, by displacing weak competitors as invader cover increased. An enhanced understanding of the multitude of ecological impacts should be a primary element of invasive plant management.
