Distribution of Macrobrachium nipponense (De Haan, 1849) In Iran (Decapoda, Palaemonidae)
Importing exotic species always has the risk of these becoming introduced pests. In a study on ponds in the Golestan Province, Iran, a number of shrimps was caught and identified as Macrobrachium nipponense. Due to the morphological similarities between M. nipponense and M. rosenbergii, it may have been erroneously introduced instead of M. rosenbergii, or it may have come along with other released aquatic animals. Until now, the species had only been reported in Iran from the southern and south-eastern parts of the Caspian Sea.
