Home range size and reproduction of female roe deer re-introduced into a Mediterranean habitat
From 1995 to 1997, 52 males and 52 females, which had been captured in the Forest ofTrois Fontaines (Northeast of France), were re-introduced into thePetit Luberon state forest (South of France). Among them 49 females (21 young [<1 year old] and 28 adults [>1 year old]) were monitored by radio-tracking. The mean annual home-range sizes (mean 95% MCP = 157 ha) were larger than those estimated in more temperate forests, but were smaller than in cultivated plains. In spring and summer, repeated visual observations allowed us to study the reproduction of the monitored docs and the summer survival of their fawns. The mean number of fawns per adult female was 1.18 (SE = 0.13) and the mean number of fawns per doe with young was 1.59 (SE = 0.09).
